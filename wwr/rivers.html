Adventure Awaits

Discover world-class rapids and unforgettable scenery on every river journey.

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Difficulty: Class I–IV

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, the Salmon River has earned the nickname “River of No Return.” Herds of elk and deer graze on the hillsides while bear and bighorn sheep are often sighted. Towering ponderosa pines line the river corridor, providing stunning campsites. The rapids vary from class I to IV and are ideal for adventurers all summer. Families with young children prefer the late summer for its slower, warmer water.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Difficulty: Class II–III

Flowing through high grey stone cliffs and vast desert terrain, the Green River’s 84-mile trip through Desolation and Gray Canyons offers breathtaking views and rich history. This route boasts fascinating geological formations, evidence of ancient civilizations, and thrilling whitewater rapids. Usually completed in 6 days, it’s a designated National Landmark for its unique and historical value.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

Difficulty: Class I–X (unique scale)

The Grand Canyon offers a river adventure unlike any other. With world-famous rapids, ancient ruins, and hidden side canyons, this trip is both thrilling and awe-inspiring. The Grand Canyon’s unique Class I–X scale allows for a wide range of rapid intensity and experiences, perfect for those wanting the ultimate whitewater journey.
